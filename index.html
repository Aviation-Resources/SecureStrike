How it works

  1. Forward a suspicious email, text, or URL to SecureStrike.
  2. AI extracts indicators, classifies the abuse, and compiles evidence.
  3. With one tap, send a standardized report to the right abuse desks.

Built to follow the proven channels (registrars, hosts, Safe Browsing, regulators) — maximum pressure, zero vigilante risk.

What you can report

  • Full URLs — find the registrar, DNS records, and hosting provider.
  • Phone numbers — DNC registry complaints, carrier lookup, and carrier reporting.
  • Emails — sender details, headers, and reputation checks.
  • Screenshots & photos — analyzed by vision AI to glean additional indicators.

Tip: include full URLs, timestamps, and screenshots to speed up action.

Report a malicious phone number

  • Block the number — use iOS “Silence Unknown Callers” or Android “Block Unknown Numbers,” or carrier tools (AT&T Call Protect, Verizon Call Filter, T‑Mobile Scam Shield).
  • Report to the FTC — ReportFraud.ftc.gov · Do Not Call complaint
  • Report to the FCC — Unwanted Calls/Texts complaint
  • Carrier spam reporting — forward the malicious text to 7726 (“SPAM”) to flag the sender with your carrier.
  • Law enforcement — for threats/harassment/fraud attempts, save logs and screenshots and contact local police.
